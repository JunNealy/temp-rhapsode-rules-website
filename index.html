Basic Rules

Rolling Mechanics

When engaging in a risky action, you will roll a number of six-sided dice (xD6). The number of dice rolled is determined by your level in the relevant skill and any bonus dice granted by equipment, circumstances, or aid from another player.

  • 1-3 counts as a MISS
  • 4 or 5 counts as 1 HIT
  • 6 counts as 2 HITS

The Game Master assigns a TARGET NUMBER from 1 to 6. This number is equal to the number of HITS needed to succeed in the action.

After rolling, count your HITS and MISSES and compare them against the TARGET NUMBER to determine the degree to which the action succeeds or fails:

  • CRITICAL SUCCESS: If you roll 2 or more sixes, you succeed critically achieving your aims and gaining an additional benefit.
  • SUCCESS: If your total hits are equal to or greater than the target number you succeed at the action.
  • PARTIAL SUCCESS: If your total hits are equal to one half the target number rounded up, you partially succeed, achieving some or all of your action with a consequence or complication.
  • FAILURE: If your total hits are lower than one half the target number, your action fails.
  • CATASTROPHE: If you failed and rolled 2 or more 1s, something goes terribly wrong.

Stakes

Every action has STAKES, representing the potential risks and rewards of the action. The GM sets the STAKES before rolling, based on the action's aim and the current circumstances.

  • LOW RISK: Safe action, mild consequences, low reward.
  • MODERATE RISK: Balanced risk and reward; failure may still be manageable.
  • HIGH RISK: Extreme risk; high reward, but failure may be catastrophic.

Raising the Stakes

You may RAISE THE STAKES before rolling, increasing the risk tier and the TARGET NUMBER by +1. Raising the stakes does not grant additional dice but increases both reward and consequences.

Sway

All players start each episode with 0 SWAY and can have a maximum of 3. Excess SWAY is wasted.

You gain +1 SWAY in the following ways:

  • When you fail a roll
  • When you invoke your Shadow
  • At the GM’s discretion for exceptional roleplaying or dramatic choices

Flourish

Before rolling, you may invoke a FACET—your own or one present in the scene:

  • Spend 1 SWAY to add +1d6 to your roll.
  • Spend 2 SWAY to add +1d6 to an ally’s roll.

Shadow

Before rolling, you may invoke a Facet to complicate your action:

  • Gain 1 SWAY and subtract 1d6 from your own roll.

Special Abilities

Special abilities are unique ways to interact with the world. Usage limits are specified per ability.

Character Turns

During your turn, you may:

  • Make one Move: Overcome, Discern, or Establish
  • Narratively move or position yourself
  • Speak freely outside of core Moves

Actions

Actions are how your character engages with uncertain outcomes. You may take one action per turn:

Overcome

Attempt to overcome an obstacle, opponent, or internal struggle.

  • Use when something opposes your intent
  • Failure should create meaningful consequences

Discern

Ask the GM questions to uncover truths about the current situation.

  • Use when understanding the scene could change your choices

Establish

Alter the fiction by introducing meaningful new details, advantages, or complications.

  • Use when you want to assert narrative agency

Facets

Facets are short, evocative descriptions of characters, scenes, or the world. They can influence actions and outcomes.

Harm

Represents physical or psychological consequences. Harm can be highlighted to impact actions or limit abilities.

Character Creation

Character Information

Record your character’s name, pronouns, and appearance.

Facets

  • Concept: Broad thematic overview
  • Motive: What drives your character
  • Bond: Ties to the world
  • Shadow: Chaotic or threatening element used to gain Sway

Approaches and Means

Approaches reflect your method of engagement; Means represent your concrete tools:

  • Cautious, Guile, Bold, Quick, Subtle, Forceful
  • Martial, Expertise, Focus, Primal, Mystic, Influence

Special Abilities & Boons

Select one at creation. More can be gained through advancement.

Harm

Each character has three harm slots. Once filled, the character is taken out.

Experience & Advancement

Mark experience flags after each episode:

  • Attended to your Bond
  • Engaged with your Shadow
  • Exemplified your Concept
  • Prioritized your Motive

Phases of Gameplay

Adventuring Phase

Time and character control are granular. Use scenes to resolve conflicts and obstacles.

Downtime Phase

Longer periods of minimal risk. Players take school or leisure actions, recover harm, or advance in skills.

Special Abilities

Abilities may be once per adventure, once per scene, or always active. Examples include:

  • Armored, Defender, Diplomat, I Know a Guy, Unfinished Business
  • Relic, Persistence, Calculated Gambit, Healer, Flashback, Sworn Enemy
  • Ambush, Berserk, Intuition, Rally, Reckless Abandon, Font of Inspiration, Deep Pockets
  • Quick Healing, Focus, Momentum, Power in Pain, Fail Forward, Chain Reaction, Good Enough, Weird, Revenge
  • Collaboration, Specialist, Always Prepared, Master of Leisure

Running the Game

The Role of the Game Master

The GM facilitates the story, challenges players, and maintains consistency. Only call for a roll when meaningful.

Framing Scenes

  1. Define the Scene’s Goal
  2. Identify Friction
  3. Highlight Special Facets
  4. Consider Outcomes
  5. Optional: Scene Hooks and Player Agency

Resources

Resources represent items, information, connections, or in-game boons. They may be limited per episode, scene, or as a numbered supply (1-6).

Glossary of Terms

  • Action: Taken when there is a possibility of success or failure.
  • Abilities: Categories governing character skills.
  • Element: A discrete person, place, or thing.
  • Facet: Short evocative description of a person, place, or thing.
  • Harm: Condition of an element when damaged.
  • Highlight: Call attention to a facet to modify rolls.
  • Push: Increase the efficacy of an action while increasing consequences.
  • Skills: Specific approaches used when taking an action.
  • Success: A numeric d6 result contributing to a roll.
  • Success (Mixed): Partial numeric result contributing half a success.
  • Sway: Meta-currency used to influence rolls.
